Draw up a map: creating a product roadmap

A thorough explanation of what a roadmap is, especially for a startup.

Your vision is constant, your strategy can change, and your roadmap needs to adapt and keep up with feedback. It's a 'living' document you should check regularly. There are many roadmap templates you can use for visualization: goal-oriented roadmaps, Jira boards, Gantt charts, or a simple timeline. Choose what works for you. Consider who you’re sharing your roadmap with (internal teams, investors, or potential customers) and the level of detail you want to convey.

  • Product vision and audience needs
    Identify your business objectives, form hypotheses, and understand your potential product audience's needs.
  • Product concept and feature selection
    Define what the product should look like and its functions.
  • Budget: identify resources
    Determine what you need to create the product - capital, people, tools?
  • Timetable: divide into phases and define time
    All about schedule in roadmap.
